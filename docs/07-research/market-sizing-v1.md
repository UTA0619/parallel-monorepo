## Part II — Top-Down Model

### Methodology

Start from total global spending on "knowing yourself better" — broadly defined — and work down to serviceable addressable market.

### Global "Self-Knowledge" Spending 2026

| Category | Spending |
|----------|---------|
| Therapy & mental health treatment | $102B |
| Personal development & coaching | $40B |
| Wellness apps & meditation | $9B |
| AI assistants (personal use) | $12B |
| Journaling, self-help books | $8B |
| Religion / spiritual practice (proxy) | ~$100B (global) |
| **Total proxy market** | **~$271B (addressable portion)** |

### Addressable Portion

Not all of this is addressable. We exclude:
- Clinical therapy (regulated, PARALLEL is not a clinical product)
- Religious/spiritual practice (cultural, not digital)

**Adjusted proxy market (2026):** $69B  
**Growth to 2030 (AI penetration):** 35% CAGR  
**Proxy market 2030:** $232B

### PARALLEL Serviceable Portion

PARALLEL is best positioned for the segment that:
- Is digitally native and tech-forward
- Pays $20–50/month for software
- Is motivated by life-path decisions and self-knowledge (not just symptom relief)

This describes approximately 18% of the proxy market:

**Top-down SAM (2030):** $232B × 18% = **$41.8B**

**10-year TAM extrapolation:** $521B
